Tf-idf by hand

Make up 5 short documents and construct the tf-idf matrix by hand for these 5 documents. Steps: 1. First make up your data. 2. Make the bag-of-words (document-term) matrix by counting the words in each document. 3. Then calculate the document frequency and inverse document frequency of each word. 4. Then divide each column by its IDF.

Use the version without log-scaling:

\[TF[d,t] = count(d,t) \,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\, IDF[t] = \frac{N}{DF[t]}\] Show your work at each step!

Which words are the most discriminative words? (Meaning, which words appear in the fewest documents, and therefore provide the most information?)
